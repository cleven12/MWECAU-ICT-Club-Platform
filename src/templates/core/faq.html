Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about MWECAU ICT Club

Membership

Who can join the ICT Club?

The ICT Club welcomes all students at Mwenge Catholic University with interest in technology. We have three membership tiers:

  • Honorary: Current ICT/Computer Science students (full access)
  • Associate: Other students interested in tech (event participation)
  • Expert: Graduated mentors and advisors

All are welcome â€“ we grow through collaboration!

How do I register as a member?
  1. Click on the "Join Club" button in the navigation menu
  2. Fill in your registration form with accurate information
  3. Select your preferred department/division
  4. Submit the form and wait for approval from leadership
  5. You'll receive a confirmation email once approved
Is membership free?

Yes! Membership in the MWECAU ICT Club is completely free. We're a student-led organization dedicated to providing free access to technology education, workshops, and project opportunities.

How long does membership approval take?

Membership applications are typically reviewed within 2-5 business days. You'll receive an email notification once your application is approved or if additional information is needed.

Departments & Divisions

What are the six departments?

The ICT Club is organized into six dynamic departments:

Programming
Cybersecurity
Networking
Computer Maintenance
Graphic Design
AI & Machine Learning
Can I join multiple departments?

Absolutely! Membership in one department doesn't restrict you from contributing to others. We strongly encourage cross-functional collaboration:

  • Select your primary department during registration
  • Participate in projects across multiple departments
  • Collaborate with members from different departments
  • Work with department leaders on interdisciplinary initiatives
How do I find my department?

After joining, you can:

  1. Visit the Departments page to browse all divisions
  2. View each department's details, members, and projects
  3. Contact the department leader for more information
  4. Attend department meetings and workshops

Projects & Events

How can I participate in projects?
  1. Join the club and get approved
  2. Visit the Projects page
  3. Browse ongoing projects in your department
  4. Express interest to the project lead
  5. Collaborate with your team to build amazing solutions
What types of projects does the club work on?

The club works on diverse projects including:

Student Information Systems
Websites & Admin Dashboards
Security Tools
Network Simulations
Robot Control Systems
UI/UX Prototypes
How often does the club hold events?

The club regularly hosts:

  • Weekly Meetings - General club updates and discussions
  • Workshops - Skills training in various technologies
  • Seminars - Guest speakers and industry professionals
  • Hackathons - Team-based development competitions
  • Social Events - Networking and team building

Check the Events page for upcoming activities.

Leadership & Roles

How can I become a leader?
  1. Be an active member for at least one semester
  2. Demonstrate commitment and leadership qualities
  3. Express interest in a specific leadership role
  4. Go through the selection process with current leadership
  5. Get elected or appointed to your position

Learn more about leadership roles on our Leadership page.

What are the main leadership positions?

The club structure includes:

Chairperson & Vice Chairperson - Overall leadership
Secretary & Assistant Secretary - Records and communication
Product Manager & Assistant PM - Projects and initiatives
Division Leaders - Lead their respective departments
